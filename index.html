Tribute to Dr. Rajendra Prasad

“The architect of the Indian Constitution and a great leader.”

About Dr. Rajendra Prasad

Dr. Rajendra Prasad (1884–1963) was an Indian independence activist, lawyer, and politician who served as the first President of India from 1950 to 1962. He was a key figure in the Indian independence movement and played a crucial role in the framing of the Indian Constitution.

  • Known for his humility and dedication to the nation.
  • Played a significant role in the Indian National Congress.
  • Instrumental in the establishment of the Indian Constitution.
  • A strong advocate for education and rural development.

Achievements

Dr. Rajendra Prasad made numerous contributions to Indian society, including:

  1. First President of India (1950-1962).
  2. Chairman of the Drafting Committee of the Indian Constitution.
  3. Played a vital role in the establishment of the Indian Institute of Agriculture.
  4. Promoted the importance of cooperative farming.

Favorite Quote

“I am convinced that the future of India depends on the education of its rural population.”
